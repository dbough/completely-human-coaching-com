About Dan Bough

I don't have it figured out. That's kind of the point.

I've spent 20 years in tech and tech leadership — building teams, navigating organizations, and trying to show up as a decent human being while doing it. I've been the guy who looks competent on the outside and is quietly drowning on the inside. I know what it's like to succeed by most measures and still feel like something important is missing.

Along the way, I became a father, deepened my faith, and found myself drawn into men's community work — spaces where people drop the performance and get honest. I write too, badly and compulsively, because putting words to things is how I make sense of them. None of this makes me an expert. It makes me someone who has done a lot of hard inner work and is still very much in it.

What I am is someone who listens well, asks questions that matter, and genuinely cares about the people I sit with. If you're looking for a thinking partner — someone to walk alongside you rather than hand you a blueprint — I'd love to talk.

What to Expect

No agenda. No homework. Just a real conversation.

A session with me isn't a seminar or a performance review. It's more like sitting across from someone who is genuinely curious about you — where you are, what's weighing on you, what you're trying to build or figure out.

  • We talk for about an hour, over video or phone, wherever you're comfortable.
  • I ask questions. You talk. I listen — actually listen, not just wait for my turn.
  • We might explore a specific situation you're in, or just start with what's on your mind.
  • I'll reflect back what I'm hearing. Sometimes that alone is clarifying.
  • You leave with your own thinking, not mine. I'm not here to fix you.

It doesn't matter if you're navigating a career transition, feeling disconnected, trying to be a better leader, or just carrying something heavy you haven't said out loud. Any of those is a good reason to reach out.
